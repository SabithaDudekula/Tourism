TOURISM PLACES

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a stunning white marble mausoleum built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. It is one of the Seven Wonders of the World and a symbol of eternal love.

Mysore Palace

Mysore Palace is a magnificent royal residence in Karnataka, known for its Indo-Saracenic architecture and grand interiors. It is one of the most visited tourist attractions in India, especially famous for its illumination during the Dussehra festival.

Charminar

The Charminar, located in Hyderabad, Telangana, is a historic monument built in 1591 by Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah. It is famous for its four grand arches and minarets, showcasing Indo-Islamic architecture. The Charminar is a cultural icon and a popular landmark in the heart of the city.

Monuments of Hampi

Hampi stands proudly as a sight of architectural wonders and an important religious centre. The architecture and construction of the place will leave you wondering how it was possible at a time with no modern machinery, tools or technology.

Amaravati

Amaravati, located in Andhra Pradesh, is a historic city on the banks of the Krishna River. It is known for its ancient Buddhist heritage, including the famous Amaravati Stupa. Today, it is also recognized as the planned capital region of Andhra Pradesh, symbolizing both tradition and progress.

Gandikota

Gandikota, in Andhra Pradesh, is known as the “Grand Canyon of India”. It is home to a historic fort, ancient temples, and stunning views of rocky landscapes. Gandikota is a popular spot for history lovers, nature enthusiasts, and adventure seekers alike.
